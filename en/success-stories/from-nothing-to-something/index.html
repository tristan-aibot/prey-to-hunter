From nothing to something: Martijn's story

At 24, Martijn worked in a warehouse, had €400 in debt and had no idea what he wanted. At 28, he had a freelance business, a flat he pays for himself and a life he consciously chose. This is his story.

The day it clicked

"I was 24 and sitting in my car in a car park after my shift. It was dark. I had just worked half an hour extra so the manager would like me — and he hadn't even said thank you. I thought: if I'm still living like this in ten years, what have I actually done?"

Martijn describes this as the moment something snapped — not dramatically, not with much emotion. Just a clear thought he couldn't stop thinking. If he changes nothing, nothing changes.

What he changed first

No big leap. No thousand-euro course. One decision: stop watching TV after 10pm and use that time to learn something. First YouTube tutorials about graphic design. Then a free Figma course. Then his first project for a cousin who wanted a logo.

"Free. Just to see if I could. I needed two weeks for a logo a professional would make in two hours. But I had made it. That was the beginning."

"It didn't start with a business plan. It started with one evening used differently."

The first client

Three months after his first project, Martijn posted something on LinkedIn. He had no large network. Eight likes. But two weeks later an old classmate called who needed a website redesign. First real client. First real payment.

"€350 for a logo. I felt like I had won the lottery — not because of the amount, but because someone had paid for my work. That was different. That was proof."

What worked — and what didn't

What worked: consistent learning, taking small projects even when they paid little, and asking questions of people who were already doing what he wanted to do. "I sent three designers a message on LinkedIn with a specific question. Two didn't respond. One gave me half an hour of advice that made my work ten times better in one day."

What didn't work: trying to perfect things before starting. "I waited two months for the 'right moment' to update my LinkedIn profile. That moment doesn't exist. You have to start and get better along the way."

The lesson for the reader

Martijn is now 28. He works full-time as a freelance designer for three fixed clients and one or two new ones per quarter. He earns more than he ever did in the warehouse. Not spectacular — but stable, and his own.

"I'm not a special case. I wasn't born smart or got lucky. I just used one evening differently. And then another. Most people wait for inspiration or the right moment. Me too, honestly. Until I stopped waiting."
